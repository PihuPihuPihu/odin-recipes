Momo with Tomato chutney


Home

Momos, a type of dumpling popular in India and Nepal, are soft, juicy, and bursting with flavor. Paired with a tangy and spicy tomato chutney, they make for the ultimate comfort food or snack. Here's how you can prepare both the momos and the chutney at home.

Ingredients

  • For the Dough:
    • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1/4 tsp salt
    • Water (as needed)
  • For the Filling
    • 1 cup finely chopped or grated cabbage
    • 1/2 cup finely chopped onions
    • 1/2 cup finely grated carrots
    • 1/2 cup finely chopped spring onions
    • 1 tbsp finely minced garlic
    • 1 tbsp finely minced ginger
    • 1 tsp soy sauce
    • 1 tsp vinegar
    • 1/2 tsp black pepper powder
    • salt to taste or 1 tsp salt
    • 1 tsp oil
    • Fresh coriander leaves (optional, for garnish)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Dough
    1. In a large mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour and salt. Gradually add water and knead to form a soft, smooth dough.
    2. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Filling
    1. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add garlic and ginger, sauté until fragrant.
    2. Add onions and stir-fry until translucent.
    3. Add cabbage, carrots, and spring onions. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes on high heat to retain the crunch.
    4. Add soy sauce, vinegar, black pepper, and salt. Mix well and cook for 1 minute. Set aside to cool.
  3. Assemble the momo with the filling and wrappers.
  4. Steam for 10-15 minutes or you can also pan-fry them.
  5. Prepare Tomato Chutney
    1. Heat oil in a pan. Add garlic, ginger, and dried red chilies. Sauté for 1 minute.
    2. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft and mushy.
    3. Transfer the mixture to a blender. Add vinegar, sugar, and salt, then blend into a smooth paste.
    4. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves if desired.
  6. Serve hot juicy momos with the Chutney!
